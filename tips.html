Learn the different components of a good workout so you can have the confidence to create a routine at home that works best for you.

Warm Up

Every workout should begin with a warm up. These movements start small and slowly become bigger as you start to raise your heart rate and work through your range of motion. You can start with a march that progressively adds more energy as you warm up. Do some shoulder shrugs and rolls. Work through your range of motion with some cross body and over the head reaches. Incorporate additional range of motion movements that include other parts of the body. Don't forget to warm up your wrists and ankles. Once your muscles are warmed up you can do a little bit of stretching to ensure your muscles are ready to go.

Strength and Endurance

Here you will incorporate your weights, resistance band, ball, cardio, and anything else you want to use during your workout. Remember to try and work every major muscle group (chest, shoulders, arms, back, core, legs, calves) so nothing gets neglected over time.

Cooldown

Cooling down is very important. It provides you with an opportunity to bring your heart rate down and start to recover from your workout. This is a good time to work on balance exercises or play a coordination game with your ball. Do something that will slow your body down and prepare you for the final part of your workout.

Stretching

Now is a good time to do some stretching. Your muscles are still warm which is the perfect time to work on your flexibility. Once you've had a chance to stretch your body out I recommend taking some long, deep breaths. Those deep breaths will leave you feeling relaxed and ready to handle whatever your day brings has in store for you.

Don't have gym equipment at home? No problem! Below are some options for equipment substitutions using items that may be more accessible at home.

Hand Weights

If you don't have hand weights at home grab some canned goods, bottled juice, or water bottles out of your cupboard. Anything will work that adds a some extra weight to your exercises.

Resistance Band

If you don't have a resistance band you can substitute a stretchy towel, shirt, or other type of fabric for some exercises. You can also imply resistance by acting like you're stretching a band. You'll provide the resistance to give yourself a good workout.

Ball

If you don't have a ball you can subsitute a stuffed animal, shirt, or towel small enough to be balled up in your hands. These options will give you something to squeeze for hand strengthening, pass back and forth for coordination, or even just hold on to during certain exercises.
